A Brief History our National Parks

The National Parks were formed because of the revolutionary idea that came from the desire to preserve the nation's natural wonders for future generations.

The concept of national parks originated in the mid-19th century. As this concept came into fruition, Americans began to realize the value of protecting the natural regions from exploitation. It was in 1864 when Congress granted Yosemite Valley and the Mariposa Grove of Giant Sequoias to California for preservation. This was the first time that a piece of land was set aside for conservation.

In 1872, Yellowstone National Park was created. This was a watershed moment as it was the first national park in the world. Significant conservationists such as John Muir strongly advocated for the wilderness to be preserved. His worked inspired others and led to Mount Rainier and Sequoia being established.

In 1916, the National Park Service or NPS, was formed. Their job was to manage the parks while also ensuring they were open and accessible to the public.

The NPS became instrumental in expanding the park system and standardizing conservation efforts across the country.

Over the decades, the National Park System expanded to include not only natural landscapes but also historical sites, and battlefields.

In 1933, President Franklin D. Roosevelt reorganized and consolidated many federally protected areas under the NPS, significantly broadening its scope.

In addition, the Wilderness Act of 1964 and the National Parks and Recreation Act of 1978 further implemented conservation and expanded protected areas.

Other Notes on Conservation Efforts

In current times, the National Park System indicates that over 400 sites exist. Because of the beliefs of advocates and leaders in environmental changes and conservation, the diverse parks stand today as a testament to the country's commitment to preservation efforts. With the continual help and support of government representatives, park rangers, volunteers, and visitors, the National Parks can continue to be enjoyed by all.

The visionary effort from past generations forges the way for present and future generations to take notes on the models of conserving natural, historical, and cultural landmarks. It is the hope of the people behind these parks, that the beauty of these natural creations will remain for many more years to come
